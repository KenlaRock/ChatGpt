# Public Design Provenance

**Status:** DESIGN_SOURCE — sanitized for public release  
**Origin date:** 2026-07-10

NullForge Simulation Lab originated from an internal research discussion about building a controlled synthetic environment for learning and validating audio-analysis methods.

The public requirements preserved here are intentionally abstracted from private workspaces and source material:

- create clean, controlled synthesis and noise environments,
- test harmonic filters audibly and visually,
- compare multiple methods against identical synthetic ground truth,
- record automation and exchange it as JSON,
- animate sources, vectors, planes and probes in physical or abstract signal space,
- condition observation or intervention on signal/geometric interactions,
- freeze sample-addressed snapshots for microbehaviour inspection,
- exaggerate visual behaviour without altering audio truth,
- keep the software and reproducible experiments open for external review.

## Promotion rules

Conversation and brainstorming are not evidence.

- An idea becomes project policy through an Architecture Decision Record.
- A claim becomes evidence through a declared reproducible experiment.
- Internal sources are replaced by synthetic or explicitly publishable fixtures before entering the public repository.

The full working discussion and private source mappings remain in the internal collaboration workspaces and are not part of the public repository.
